# GTM & Sales Playbook

## Core Rule
**Revenue solves most startup problems.** When in doubt, go sell something — today.

---

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Define before any outreach. Answer these:

```
1. Industry/Vertical: [What kind of company/person?]
2. Size: [Revenue, headcount, or other proxy]
3. Pain: [What specific problem do they have?]
4. Trigger: [What event makes them ready to buy NOW?]
5. Anti-ICP: [Who should we NOT sell to?]
```

Write your ICP in one sentence:
> "[Title] at [company type] who [trigger event] and struggles with [pain] — they currently [workaround]."

## Outreach Frameworks

### Cold Email (3-Email Sequence)

**Email 1 — The Hook**
```
Subject: [Specific result] for [their company type]

Hi [Name],

[1 sentence personal relevance — why them, why now.]

[Company] helps [ICP] [specific outcome] — [one proof point].

Worth a quick call to see if it's a fit?

[Name]
```

**Email 2 — The Value Add (3 days later)**
```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Wanted to share [relevant resource / insight / case study] in case it's useful regardless of timing.

Still happy to connect if the moment is right.
```

**Email 3 — The Close (5 days later)**
```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Last note — didn't want to keep bothering you.

If timing ever works, [calendly link or simple ask].

Either way, rooting for you.
```

### Cold DM (LinkedIn / Slack / Community)
```
Hi [Name] — saw [specific thing about them / their work].

Quick question: [problem-focused question, not a pitch].

Happy to share what we've learned if useful.
```

## Discovery Call Framework (30 min)

```
0:00 — Agenda + permission (2 min)
0:02 — Their situation: "Tell me about [relevant context]" (8 min)
0:10 — The problem: "What's the hardest part of [topic]?" (7 min)
0:17 — Current solution: "What are you doing now?" (5 min)
0:22 — The ask: Pitch + next step (5 min)
0:27 — Close: "Does this make sense to move forward?" (3 min)
```

**Never pitch before 20 minutes in.**

## Pricing Strategy

### 3 Pricing Models

| Model | Best For | Risk |
|-------|----------|------|
| Flat monthly | Simple SaaS, early stage | Leaves value on table |
| Usage-based | API, consumption products | Unpredictable for customer |
| Outcome-based | High-value B2B, services | Hard to measure |

### Pricing Rules
- **Start higher than you think.** It's easier to discount than to raise.
- **Anchor on value, not cost.** What is the problem worth to them?
- **Always offer 3 tiers.** Middle tier gets chosen most often.
- **Don't negotiate on price — negotiate on scope.**
- **Test pricing with real offers before you build pricing pages.**

### Willingness to Pay Test
Ask: *"If this costs $X/month, does that work for you?"*  
Watch body language / hesitation. That's real data.

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Too expensive" | "What would make it worth it?" or "Compared to what?" |
| "We already have something" | "What does it not do that you wish it did?" |
| "Let me think about it" | "What would help you decide?" |
| "Not right now" | "When would be right? Can we put something on the calendar?" |
| "Send me more info" | "What specifically would help you evaluate this?" |
| "I need to check with my team" | "Who else should be on the next call?" |

## Pipeline Tracking (Minimum Viable CRM)

Track in a spreadsheet with columns:

```
Name | Company | Stage | Last Contact | Next Action | Deal Size | Notes
```

**Stages:**
1. Lead (identified)
2. Contacted (reached out)
3. Conversation (had a call)
4. Proposal sent
5. Negotiating
6. Closed Won / Closed Lost

Review pipeline every Friday.

---

## Closing Techniques

- **Assumptive close:** "Should I send over the agreement today or Monday?"
- **Summary close:** "Based on what you've shared, [X] solves [Y]. Should we move forward?"
- **Deadline close:** "We have onboarding slots open this month — want to lock one in?"
- **Trial close:** "If we can solve [objection], is there anything else holding you back?"

**Ask for the close. Explicitly. Every time.**

## Revenue Goals Framework

Work backward:

```
Monthly revenue goal: $X
Average deal size: $Y
Deals needed: X ÷ Y = Z
Conversion rate from conversation: ~20–30%
Conversations needed: Z × 4 = W
Outreach needed (at ~10% reply rate): W × 10
```

This tells you exactly how many messages to send per week.
