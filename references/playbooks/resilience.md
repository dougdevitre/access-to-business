# Resilience Playbook

## Core Rule
**The startup is the test. You are not the startup.**  
Founders who survive long enough to find PMF usually win. The main thing is staying in the game.

---

## Rejection Protocol

When you get a "no" — from a customer, investor, partner, or employer:

**Step 1 — Acknowledge it (30 seconds):**  
Say to yourself: "That's data."

**Step 2 — Extract the signal:**  
- Was this a timing issue, a fit issue, or a message issue?
- Would a different customer say yes?
- Would a different framing change the outcome?

**Step 3 — Categorize it:**
- **Useful rejection:** Tells you what to fix
- **Expected rejection:** Part of the funnel, doesn't mean anything
- **Irrelevant rejection:** Wrong customer, wrong context — ignore

**Step 4 — Next action in 5 minutes:**  
Send the next outreach. File the feedback. Do not spiral.

## Reframing Failure

| What happened | What it means (founder reframe) |
|---------------|--------------------------------|
| Lost a customer | Got data on what to fix |
| Investor passed | Wrong fit — not a referendum on the company |
| Feature flopped | Learned what users don't want |
| Co-founder left | Painful, survivable — many great companies went through this |
| Competitor launched | Validates the market exists |
| Revenue missed | Tells you what assumptions were wrong |
| Team member quit | Culture signal or growth opportunity |

## Pivot vs Persevere Framework

Ask these questions when something isn't working:

```
1. Is the problem real? (customers still have it)
   YES → continue to 2 | NO → pivot problem

2. Is our solution solving it? (retention, satisfaction)
   YES → continue to 3 | NO → pivot solution

3. Can we reach our customers at viable cost?
   YES → continue to 4 | NO → pivot channel

4. Will they pay what we need to be sustainable?
   YES → keep going | NO → pivot model or pricing
```

**Pivot ≠ failure.** Slack, Instagram, YouTube, Twitter — all pivots.  
**Panic pivot = failure.** Pivot based on data, not fear.

## Founder Burnout Prevention

### Warning Signs
- Dreading work you used to enjoy
- Decision fatigue starting at 9am
- Irritability with team or co-founder
- Sleeping too much or too little
- Detachment from the mission ("why are we doing this?")

### Recovery Protocol
1. **Identify the root cause:** Is it the work, a relationship, the direction, or the pace?
2. **Protect one block of protected time** per day that isn't meetings or email
3. **Audit your calendar:** Cut 2 recurring meetings this week
4. **Talk to someone:** Another founder, a therapist, a mentor — not just your co-founder
5. **Reconnect with a customer:** Remind yourself why this matters

### Sustainable Habits (Not optional)
- Sleep 7+ hours. Decisions degrade without it.
- Exercise 3x per week minimum. Sedentary = foggy.
- One full day off per week. Not "light work Sunday" — off.
- Monthly solo reflection: Where am I? Where is the company? Are they the same direction?

## Managing Uncertainty

Startup = permanent ambiguity. Get comfortable with these:

- **You will not have enough information.** Decide anyway.
- **You will be wrong regularly.** Adjust quickly.
- **The plan will change.** Write it down anyway.
- **You don't know when it will work.** Keep showing up.

**Tactics for navigating uncertainty:**
- Set 90-day milestones instead of 5-year plans
- Make reversible decisions fast; slow down only for irreversible ones
- Write down your assumptions before executing — check them after
- Find 1–2 other founders to talk to regularly (peer group > mentor)

## When to Quit vs When to Persist

**Signals to persist:**
- Customers love it but growth is slow (distribution problem, not product problem)
- The core insight is still valid even if the execution needs work
- You have runway and real learning happening
- You still believe in the problem

**Signals to seriously reconsider:**
- No customers will pay, regardless of version or price
- The market fundamentally changed and the problem is gone
- The founding team is broken beyond repair
- You no longer believe in the mission
- You've run out of ways to test the core hypothesis

**One year of real effort before concluding it can't work.** But don't confuse persistence with denial.
